### Game Design: Bitcoin Origins Quiz for Boomers

#### Game Overview
- **Title**: Bitcoin Basics: The Story of Digital Money
- **Target Audience**: Baby Boomers (born 1946–1964), with basic computer skills but limited crypto knowledge. Use clear language, analogies (e.g., blockchain as a "public ledger"), large fonts, and high-contrast colors for accessibility.
- **Platform**: Web-based on Replit, built with HTML/CSS/JavaScript. Deploy as a static site or lightweight Node.js app. Use local storage for progress/scores.
- **Objective**: Educate players on Bitcoin’s origins, events, regulations, and modern uses through 20 multiple-choice questions, with concise hints and ads for monetization.
- **Structure**:
  - **Intro Screen**: Welcome message: “Bitcoin is like digital cash invented in 2008, similar to email replacing letters. Ready to learn its story?”
  - **Quiz**: 20 questions in 4 sections (5 each): Origins & Satoshi, Key Events & Scandals, Regulations & Challenges, Modern Adoption.
  - **Hints**: Short (~37–50 words) hints displayed before answering via a “Show Hint” button, using analogies to teach Boomers.
  - **Ads**: Display after every 2–3 questions (e.g., banner, 5–10-second video, or affiliate links like “Explore senior-friendly crypto apps!”) via iframes or placeholders for Google AdSense.
  - **Feedback**: After each answer, show correct/incorrect with a brief explanation reinforcing the hint.
  - **End Screen**: Show score (10 points per correct answer), Bitcoin Knowledge Level (0–10: Novice, 11–15: Enthusiast, 16–20: Guru), takeaways, and replay/share option.
- **Mechanics**:
  - Multiple-choice: 4 options per question, one correct.
  - Hints: Displayed on button click before answering.
  - Progression: Linear with progress bar. Optional: 30-second timer per question for gentle urgency.
  - Accessibility: Large text, keyboard navigation, high-contrast mode, text-to-speech compatibility.
- **Implementation for Replit Agent**:
  - Store questions in a JavaScript array: `{question: "...", options: ["A...", "B...", "C...", "D..."], correct: "A", explanation: "...", hint: "..."}`.
  - Use DOM manipulation to render questions/hints dynamically.
  - Ads: Implement with `setTimeout` or event listeners to show after answers (e.g., `<div id="ad-placeholder">`).
  - Styling: Use Bootstrap for responsive, Boomer-friendly UI.
  - Monetization: Track ad impressions via console logs; integrate ad scripts (e.g., AdSense or affiliate APIs).
  - Optional: Add sound effects (e.g., correct answer chime) or images (e.g., Bitcoin logo) via Replit file uploads.

##### Section 1: Origins & Satoshi (Questions 1–5)
1. **Question**: Who is credited with inventing Bitcoin, and what was their famous publication?  
   **Options**:  
   A) Elon Musk, the Tesla Manifesto  
   B) Satoshi Nakamoto, the Bitcoin Whitepaper  
   C) Bill Gates, the Microsoft Money Paper  
   D) Warren Buffett, the Investment Ledger  
   **Correct**: B  
   **Explanation**: Satoshi Nakamoto, a mysterious figure, created Bitcoin and published the Bitcoin Whitepaper in 2008, outlining a digital currency without banks – like email bypassing post offices.  
   **Hint**: Imagine inventing money that works online without banks. In 2008, someone using a fake name, like a mystery author, shared a plan called a “whitepaper” – a blueprint for Bitcoin. This person, Satoshi Nakamoto, sparked a revolution. Who wrote this famous document?

   **Correct**: A  
   **Explanation**: The Genesis Block, mined on January 3, 2009, was Bitcoin’s first transaction record, launching the blockchain – like the opening page of a public ledger.  
   **Hint**: Bitcoin’s blockchain is like a public notebook for transactions. Its first page, the Genesis Block, started it all after the 2008 financial crisis. It was “mined” by solving a math puzzle, like unlocking a safe, soon after Bitcoin’s plan was shared. When did this happen?
