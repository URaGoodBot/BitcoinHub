Building a stock and crypto analysis tool that leverages Federal Reserve information, historical chart patterns, technical analysis, and AI-driven insights—while supporting text-based queries and advanced visualizations—echoes historical economic paradoxes like Triffin's dilemma. In the 1960s, economist Robert Triffin highlighted how the U.S. dollar's dominance as the world's reserve currency created inherent conflicts: the need for U.S. trade deficits to supply global liquidity inevitably undermined confidence in the dollar, exposing flaws in the Bretton Woods system that led to its collapse in 1971. Today, similar tensions persist in our fiat-based monetary framework, where central bank policies (like the Fed's quantitative easing) inflate asset bubbles in stocks and crypto, distort historical patterns, and fuel volatility that AI tools can help dissect. Free, no-signup APIs without API keys or subscriptions are rare due to data costs and abuse prevention, but several open endpoints exist for real-time/historical prices, economic indicators, and related data. These can feed your tool's algorithms for pattern recognition (e.g., via time-series data), technical indicators (computed client-side from raw feeds), and AI models trained on public datasets. Below, I've grouped them into categories, focusing on those verifiable as truly unrestricted based on public documentation and usage examples. Note that rate limits or terms may apply, and for TA/AI, you'll often pull raw data to process locally (e.g., using libraries like TA-Lib for indicators or scikit-learn for learning on historical series).

### Economic Data APIs (FED-Related and Macro Indicators)
These provide federal financial metrics, interest rates, debt, and global econ data—key for contextualizing Fed policies akin to how Triffin's insights revealed systemic imbalances in U.S. monetary expansion.

| API Name | Description | Key Endpoints/Usage | Source |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|--------|
| U.S. Treasury Fiscal Data | Open access to U.S. federal debt, interest rates, revenue/spending, and savings bonds data (e.g., Debt to the Penny, average interest rates). | Econdb | Global macroeconomic indicators (e.g., GDP, inflation, unemployment) from sources like World Bank and IMF; historical series for pattern analysis in AI models. | https://www.econdb.com/api/series/?page_size=5&search=US+GDP | https://www.econdb.com/api/  |

### Stock Market APIs
These offer real-time/historical prices and sentiment data for stocks, enabling chart pattern detection (e.g., head-and-shoulders via time-series) and technical analysis (compute RSI/MACD from OHLC data). Much like the gold standard's rigidity exposed in Triffin's era, these help reveal how modern asset markets react to monetary distortions.
